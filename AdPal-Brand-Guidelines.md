# AdPal Brand & Web Design Guidelines

## 1. Brand Essence & Core Concept

**The Theme:** "Value through Refraction." The website should visually communicate the journey from a static, physical Web2 space (the E-ink screen) into a dynamic, rewarding Web3 ecosystem.

**The Vibe:** Premium Fintech meets cutting-edge Web3. It should feel structural, secure, and grounded in physical hardware, but capable of digital magic. Avoid gamified, cartoonish elements (no floating coins or cartoon blocks).

## 2. Color Palette

The color system relies on high contrast, using the starkness of E-ink to make the Web3 crypto elements pop.

### The Foundation (Hardware & Structure):

- **Pure White (#FFFFFF):** Used for primary backgrounds, ensuring a clean, modern, and high-end tech feel.
- **Matte Obsidian (#1A1A1A to #2C2C2C):** Used for heavy typography, dark mode sections, and structural UI elements.
- **E-Ink Gray (#E0E0E0):** Used for borders, inactive states, and subtle background separations.

### The Refraction (Web3 Value & CTAs):

- **Cyber Mint (#00FFC2):** Represents energy, successful transactions, and the entry point of value.
- **Electric Violet (#BC13FE):** Represents the depth of the blockchain, premium rewards, and the Web3 ecosystem.
- **The Prism Gradient:** A seamless transition from Cyber Mint to Electric Violet. This is your primary brand asset for drawing attention.

## 3. Typography

The typography needs to mirror the "Folded A"—bold, structural, and unbreakable, yet friendly and legible.

- **Primary Headings:** A geometric sans-serif that feels architectural and wide. (e.g., Space Grotesk, Syne, or Clash Display). Keep font weights bold and tightly tracked to convey security.
- **Body Copy:** A clean, highly legible neo-grotesque sans-serif for optimal readability. (e.g., Inter, Roobert, or SF Pro).

## 4. UI/UX Elements & Textures

Translate the duality of the logo's materials directly into your website's interface components.

### "Matte Obsidian" Elements (The Exterior):

- Use dark, solid cards or containers with a very subtle noise/grain overlay in the CSS to mimic the sandblasted aluminum/velvety feel of the physical case.
- Keep borders sharp or very slightly rounded (no massive, bubbly border-radiuses).

### "Holographic Glass" Elements (The Interior):

- Use Glassmorphism selectively for high-value areas (e.g., reward dashboards, user balances, or feature highlights).
- Primary Call-to-Action (CTA) buttons should feature the Prism Gradient, but heavily utilize transparency, background blur, and a subtle glowing drop-shadow.

### The "Data Spark" (Micro-interactions):

- Hover states on buttons or cards shouldn't just change color; they should feature a sharp, fast "glint" or white sweeping highlight across the element, representing the flash of casting to the E-ink screen and the signing of an on-chain transaction.

## 5. Imagery & Visuals

- **Product Photography:** The E-ink phone case should be shown in realistic, physical environments with clean lighting and soft, grounded drop shadows (just like the logo rendering).
- **Abstract Graphics:** When visualizing the AdPal protocol or data flow, use continuous, folding ribbons or Mobius strip shapes. Avoid disconnected nodes or scattered dots; everything should feel like a single, unbreakable infrastructure.

## 6. Motion & Animation

- **The Fold:** Page transitions or scrolling animations should mimic a folding motion. Instead of fading in, elements can "unfold" or hinge into place, revealing the vibrant gradient beneath a dark exterior.
- **Snappy Precision:** Animations should not be floaty or slow. They should be crisp, deliberate, and physical—like snapping a premium case onto a phone.
